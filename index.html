A Swallow on the Wall, Temperance Downing / Last night I heard you crying. You attached yourself to the swallow that sang outside your window. So when a feather was left in its place, your hair became matted, you stopped wearing your favorite black boots. Because the last time you wore them, that swallow still existed. You began to despise the birds who sang a sonnet every morning. Because those, were not your swallow. The skin around your nose became raw, and each tissue was labelled and hung. June nineteenth, the day you hung that feather on the wall.
